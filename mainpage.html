Fun Running Tips!
Sample Text

There are many different lengths of races.

  • 5k (3.1 miles)
    • Great race for beginners.
    • Fun event, many include t-shirt and post-race party that has live music, food and drinks!
    • Average Cost $32.
    • These races are in almost all cities, and very popular.
  • 10k (6.2 miles)
    • Great race for a challenge when experimenting with higher mileage.
    • Average cost: $45.
  • Quarter-Marathon (6.55 miles)
    • Not the most popular race, but are many times associated with a full or half marathon.
  • 15k (9.3 miles)
    • Great race for people looking to add distance.
    • Many times are paired with a 5K race.
    • Example of a good event: Hot Chocolate 5k/15K (with a great jacket- however beware the high entry fee)
  • Half-Marathon (13.1 miles)
    • Personal favorite race - great for long distance, but not super intense training, and you will be able to walk the next day.
    • Training can take 3mo, but will not drastically change your regular work/excercise/social routine.
    • A sample training schedule is here .
    • My personal training hint - get up t 10 miles injury-free. Once you can run 10 miles, you can run a half.
    • Average Cost: $95.
  • Marathon (26.2 miles)
    • THE race on most runners bucket list.
    • An amazing opportunity and feeling when you finish your first - you will wow your friends - soak it up, you deserve it!
    • Training (3-4 mo) will be much different than your regular routine. Long runs may affect your regular routine, and you will need to eat and sleep more.
    • A good training schedule can be found here.
    • Average Cost: $125.
  • Ultra-Marathon (Varies, 26.2+ miles)
    • I have never run an ultra, so my knowledge is limited. (Sorry!)
    • 50K Average Cost: $90.
    • 100K Average Cost: $165.
    • 100mi Average Cost: $180.
